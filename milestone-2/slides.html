Distribution Models

  • Freemium,Prosumer, and Software as a Service (SaaS)

  • Freemium: Free video distribution, with advanced options avaiable upon payment(s). Usually users are moreso consumers.

  • Prosumer: Peer-to-peer video sharing, where users both create and view video.

  • Saas: I am referring to the software that are only avaiable upon payment or subscription to the service.

Distribution Model Grouping

  • The animation shows which platforms are which type of distribution.

  • Platforms involve one or more of the distribution models.

Technical Means

  • Platform has already been defined, a prosumer platform is a platform that offer prosumer activity. These category makes differentiation between the "average person's" production with business production

  • On the other end of the spectrum is the business content mangement system (CMS). Business video CMS is another type of platform, most likely classfiable as a SaaS. A CMS is a essentially a cloud storage platform with flexible distribution parameters. Videos can be stored (hosted) whilst controlling access. Video CMS allows for content distributioned to be selectively chosen.

  • The "picture frame" in which an Internet user is viewing the "motion picture" is a web video player. Web video players play videos on an Internet browser.

  • The "picture frame" in which a digital video is viewed is an home video players. Home video players play videos on a online or offline computer.

Technical Means

  • Video CMS platform is an extension of the traditional producer to consumer relationship. Video CMS have their own controlled video players. Prosumer platforms, web video players, and home video players are a part of the new wave of Internet video.
